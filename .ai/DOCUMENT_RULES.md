# Documentation Rules

## One source of truth

- Foundation documents define identity, vision, philosophy, and principles.
- Architecture documents define boundaries, ownership, and interactions.
- Specifications define concrete behavior and acceptance criteria.
- ADRs and decision records explain significant accepted choices and their rationale.
- Roadmaps define sequencing and milestones.
- Historical documents record past work; they do not override active decisions.

Do not duplicate a concept across categories when a link or short reference will do.

## Safe maintenance

- Preserve existing documents unless the maintainer explicitly approves a replacement or deletion.
- Explain proposed moves, consolidations, or deletions before performing them.
- Do not silently treat a template, roadmap item, or historical note as an accepted decision.
- Keep links relative to the file that contains them and verify the target exists.
- Record contradictions and missing authority rather than inventing a resolution.
