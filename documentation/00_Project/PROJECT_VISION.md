# 1. Purpose

The Project Vision defines the long-term direction of the Living Systems Project.

This document describes what the project is ultimately trying to become, the experiences it aims to create, and the principles that guide future growth.

The vision exists beyond individual features, mechanics, or implementations.

---

# 2. The Vision

The Living Systems Project aims to create Minecraft experiences where systems feel alive, adaptive, and interconnected.

The goal is to transform the Minecraft world from a collection of separate mechanics into an environment where intelligent systems exist, evolve, and interact.

Players should feel like they are participating in a living world rather than simply using a collection of tools.

---

# 3. The Desired Player Experience

A successful Living Systems experience should create moments where players think:

- "This world is doing something without me."
- "I influenced this system, but I don't completely control it."
- "This creature has a purpose beyond existing as a mob."
- "My choices changed how this ecosystem developed."

The player should feel like:

- An explorer
- A partner
- A leader
- A participant

Not simply:

- A controller
- A spectator
- A button presser

---

# 4. A Living World Philosophy

Traditional Minecraft systems are often designed around direct player interaction.

The Living Systems Project aims to expand that idea by creating systems that:

- Have internal goals
- Respond to changing conditions
- Adapt over time
- Remember previous events
- Influence their surroundings

The world should continue to function even when the player is not directly involved.

---

# 5. Long-Term Goals

## 5.1 Create Autonomous Ecosystems

Future systems should be capable of:

- Existing independently
- Responding to environmental changes
- Developing internal structures
- Creating relationships between entities

---

## 5.2 Build Intelligent Entity Frameworks

Entities should become more than simple scripted actors.

Future entities should support:

- Individual identity
- Roles
- Memory
- Goals
- Relationships
- Collective behavior

---

## 5.3 Create Emergent Gameplay

The project should encourage gameplay that emerges naturally from interactions between systems.

Examples:

- A Hive expands because resources are available.
- Drones specialize because of colony needs.
- Players influence development through choices.
- Environmental changes affect behavior.

---

# 6. HiveMind's Role in the Vision

HiveMind is the first major step toward the Living Systems vision.

It establishes the foundation for:

- Autonomous agents
- Collective intelligence
- System-based gameplay
- Adaptive behavior

HiveMind is not simply a "drone mod."

It is a testbed for creating living systems within Minecraft.

---

# 7. Future Expansion

The Living Systems Project may eventually expand into additional systems.

Potential future directions:

## Ecosystems

Living environments with:

- Natural relationships
- Resource cycles
- Population changes

---

## Intelligent Civilizations

Systems capable of:

- Growth
- Organization
- Conflict
- Cooperation

---

## Advanced World Interaction

Systems that influence:

- Terrain
- Resources
- Structures
- Player environments

---

# 8. What Success Looks Like

The Living Systems Project succeeds when:

## The World Feels Alive

Systems operate naturally without requiring constant player input.

---

## Players Create Stories

The project creates experiences that cannot be fully predicted.

---

## Systems Work Together

Individual mechanics become more valuable because of their relationships with other systems.

---

## The Architecture Supports Growth

The project can continue expanding without requiring major rewrites.

---

# 9. Vision Boundaries

The project should avoid becoming:

## A Complete Automation Replacement

Automation should enhance gameplay, not remove interaction.

---

## A Collection of Unrelated Features

Every addition should support the larger ecosystem.

---

## A Pure Simulation Engine

The project should remain a Minecraft experience first.

Systems exist to create gameplay, not complexity for its own sake.

---

# 10. Final Vision Statement

The Living Systems Project seeks to create a Minecraft world where intelligent systems exist alongside the player, where actions have consequences, and where unexpected stories emerge naturally from the interaction between players, entities, and environments.

The goal is not to build a world that plays itself.

The goal is to build a world that feels alive.
