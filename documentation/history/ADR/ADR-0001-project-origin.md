# ADR-0001 — Project Origin

**Project:** Living Systems Project (LSP)  
**Module:** HiveMind  
**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

---

# Context

The Living Systems Project began from the goal of creating Minecraft systems that feel more alive than traditional scripted mechanics.

Traditional Minecraft entities generally operate through limited behaviors:

- Basic movement
- Simple reactions
- Fixed interactions

While effective, these systems often feel disconnected from one another.

The goal of LSP was to explore a different approach:

Creating interconnected systems where entities, environments, and player actions influence one another.

---

# Decision

The project would be built around the concept of "living systems."

Instead of creating isolated features, development would focus on:

- Autonomous entities
- Interconnected mechanics
- Emergent behavior
- Persistent world interaction

The first implementation of this philosophy would become HiveMind.

---

# Alternatives Considered

## Traditional Content Mod Approach

Rejected.

Reason:

This approach focuses primarily on adding:

- New mobs
- Items
- Blocks
- Recipes

While valuable, it does not fulfill the project's long-term goal.

---

## Fully Scripted Behavior

Rejected.

Reason:

Highly scripted systems create predictable outcomes and limit emergent gameplay.

---

# Consequences

## Positive

- Establishes a clear design direction.
- Encourages modular architecture.
- Creates room for future expansion.

## Negative

- Requires more planning.
- Requires stronger documentation.
- Systems may take longer to develop.
